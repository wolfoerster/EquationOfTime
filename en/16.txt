If we were to correct each rise and set time by the corresponding difference of 
noon, we would see that both latest rise and earliest set would happen on 
December 21, the time of the winter solstice.

So how long is a day? Obviously it is something about 24 hours, but sometimes 
it's a few seconds more, and sometimes it's less. 

Let's change the world a little bit and make each day last exactly 24 hours!
All we have to do for this is to remove the axial tilt and let the earth orbit
the sun on a perfect circle (which is only nearly true in reality).
